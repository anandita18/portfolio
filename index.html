    These are some of the UX Projects I have worked on

    UX Research: Connecting resources for refugees

    For this project, our client was Office of Global Michigan. This office supports immigrant and refugee integration initiatives for the state. Currently, there are voids in the information that the Office of Global Michigan is collecting on refugee services. In order to provide better support, they need to find out how refugees are accessing mainstream services and the challenges they face when navigating the systems that host these services.

    Systems Design: Paperless Receipts

    Receipts are a commonly overlooked part of a customer’s shopping experience, and often end up lost or in the garbage. This makes returns, exchanges, and any other post transaction interactions more difficult for both the business and the consumers. With the increasing popularity of Apple Pay, consumers are beginning to use mobile payments more often. With the new interaction of mobile devices with POS systems, there are many new opportunities to be explored. We believe we have a solution to make Apple Pay more intuitive and seamless for both the consumer and the business by essentially eliminating paper receipts. In response to this problem, our team developed a system with the vision of moving away from paper receipts, in favor of virtual receipts. Sponsored by Apple’s virtual payment division, Apply Pay, our team developed this system with the intentions of it becoming an eventual local feature of Apple Pay.

    UX Design: Safety Nav

    My first time solo-traveling, and navigating through an unfamiliar city wasn’t easy. Seeing multiple crime alerts, and reading horror stories online made it difficult to predict which areas to avoid. Therefore, I decided to explore the area of travel safety. With the project timeline in mind and with a focus on travelers who choose to walk, I selected young solo travelers between the ages of 18-35 as my primary audience. I developed a mobile prototype as my final project for the School of Information Course "SI 582: Introduction to Interaction Design." Throughout this course, alongside fundamental readings about the design process and best practices, I worked towards developing a final, high-fidelity prototype after background research, user testing, and decision iteration. From researching to sketching to prototyping, I learned about contemporary perspectives on interaction design through both discussion of readings in lectures and peer collaboration.
